Frequently Asked Questions

Are reusable straws really better for the environment?

Yes. A single stainless steel straw replaces thousands of disposable straws over its lifetime. Even accounting for manufacturing energy, most reusable straws become carbon-positive after just 2-3 weeks of daily use compared to single-use plastic.

How do you clean reusable straws?

Most reusable straws come with a thin cleaning brush. Simply run the brush through with warm soapy water after each use. Silicone, steel, and glass straws are also dishwasher safe. For bamboo, hand wash and air dry to prevent mold.

Are metal straws safe for teeth?

Metal straws are safe for most adults but can pose a risk if you bite down hard. They're not recommended for young children, people with dental implants, or anyone who tends to chew on straws. Silicone is a safer alternative for these groups.

Which reusable straw is best for hot drinks?

Glass and silicone straws are the best options for hot drinks. Metal straws conduct heat and can burn your lips. Bamboo, paper, and wheat straws aren't designed for hot beverages.

Do paper straws contain PFAS chemicals?

A 2023 study found PFAS (per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances) in many paper straw brands. Look for brands that are explicitly PFAS-free and certified by third-party testing organizations. This is another reason many people prefer fully reusable options.

Can I bring reusable straws to restaurants?

Absolutely! Many restaurants welcome customers who bring their own straws. A collapsible stainless steel straw with a keychain case makes this especially easy. Some coffee shops even offer small discounts for bringing reusable accessories.

What's the most affordable eco-friendly straw option?

Stainless steel straws offer the best long-term value at $6-12 for a set that lasts a lifetime. For single-use events, wheat straws at around $0.08-0.14 each are the most affordable biodegradable option.
